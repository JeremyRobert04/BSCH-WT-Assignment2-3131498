A little history<\/strong><\/h3>\n\n\n\n
\n
\n

To understand a sport, you need to know its history. Basketball was born in the winter of 1891, in Springfield, USA. James Naismith (1861-1939), a teacher at the YMCA (Young Men’s Christian Association) Normal School, came up with the idea of a new indoor sport. The idea was to have a new, fun, educational activity that favored skill over strength. It was a success, and the sport quickly spread to the United States and then the rest of the world. The first match in Europe was played on December 27 1893 in Paris at the rue de Tr\u00e9vise.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Despite its early appearance, it took time for basketball to develop in France. In 1921, it was first managed by a commission of the F\u00e9d\u00e9ration Fran\u00e7aise d’Athl\u00e9tisme, which became the F\u00e9d\u00e9ration Fran\u00e7aise d’Athl\u00e9tisme et de Basket-Ball in 1929. The FFBB was founded in 1932.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n
