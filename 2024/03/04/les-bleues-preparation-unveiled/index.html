Les Bleues’ preparation unveiled

With 144 days to go until the start of the Paris 2024 Olympic Games (July 26 to August 11), Alain Contensoux (National Technical Director), Jacques Commères (Director of Performance and the French Teams) and Céline Dumerc (General Manager of the French Women’s Team) on Monday unveiled the summer program for the French Women’s Team, which will meet from June 6 and feature five top-level preparatory matches.

Les Bleues know their preparation program for the Paris 2024 Olympic Games. Qualified as the competition’s host nation, the Tricolores will meet again from Thursday June 6 at INSEP (Paris) with the traditional medical check-ups and Media Day. Jean-Aimé Toupane’s players will then head to Anglet (64) for an initial training camp from June 9 to 19.

After a few days’ rest, the players will meet up again in La Roche-sur-Yon (85) on June 23 for the second part of their preparation, which will see them face Finland twice at the Vendéspace (4,000 seats), on Saturday June 29 and Monday July 1.

The Tricolores will then gather in Lyon (69) on July 6 for a new training camp, culminating in a high-level match against Serbia on Friday July 12 at the LDLC Arena (12,000 seats), also the scene of a France-Serbia men’s match on the same day.

Finally, Les Bleues will conclude their preparations with a final training camp in Reims (51) from July 16, where they will play their final warm-up matches before the Olympic Games at the Reims Arena (5,500 seats) on Friday July 19 and Sunday July 21, in a 4-team tournament featuring France, Belgium (European champions in 2023), China (world runners-up in 2022) and Japan (Olympic runners-up in 2021). The direct confrontations between these four teams will be determined following the draw for the Olympic Games, which will take place at FIBA headquarters in Geneva (Switzerland) on March 19.

The French women’s team will then head for Lille on Tuesday July 23 to take up residence in the Lille Olympic Village, just a few days before the start of the competition.

Tickets for Les Bleues’ preparatory matches will go on sale at the end of March/beginning of April.
